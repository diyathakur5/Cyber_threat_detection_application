Precautions for Spam Messages

  1. Don't Click Suspicious Links
    Avoid clicking on any links from unknown or untrusted sources.
    These links may lead to phishing sites or install malware.
  2. Verify the Sender
    Check if the number or email is genuine.
    Look for slight changes like extra letters or strange domains (e.g., amaz0n.com instead of amazon.com).
  3. Never Share Personal Info
    Don’t share OTPs, bank details, passwords, or Aadhaar numbers via messages.
    No legit organization will ask for this info over SMS or chat.
  4. Use Spam Filters
    Enable spam filters in your SMS or messaging apps.
    Apps like Truecaller or Google Messages can automatically block known spammers.
  5. Report Spam
    Use in-app options to report as spam or block the number.
    You can also report spam to 1909 (TRAI) in India.
  6. Avoid Shortened URLs
    URLs like bit.ly/xyz can hide the final destination.
    Use a URL expander service or avoid them entirely unless verified.
  7. Check for Urgency Tactics
    Messages with urgency like "Act Now!", "Limited Offer", "Your account will be blocked", etc., are red flags.
  8. Keep Software Updated
    Regularly update your phone's OS and apps to protect from vulnerabilities used by spammers.
  9. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
    Use 2FA on your accounts to add an extra layer of security.
  10. Educate Yourself & Others
    Share knowledge with friends and family to prevent them from becoming victims.
